# Failed Legislation Summary (118th Congress)

This summary tracks the furthest stage reached by each bill and the proximate reason it failed in the 118th Congress.

## S.1171 - Ending Trading and Holdings In Congressional Stocks (ETHICS) Act

- Most advanced version: **Reported in Senate (RS)**
- Source file: `failed-legislation/118th-congress-s1171-ethics-act-rs.xml`
- Legislative progress:
  - Introduced in Senate
  - Referred to Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee
  - Reported by committee (S. Rept. 118-309)
  - Placed on Senate Legislative Calendar (Calendar No. 729)
- How far it got: **Ready for potential Senate floor consideration, but no floor vote occurred.**
- Why it failed:
  - Senate leadership did not bring it to a floor vote before the 118th Congress ended.
  - Under congressional rules, unpassed bills expire at the end of a Congress, so it died on adjournment.

## S.2773 - Ban Congressional Stock Trading Act

- Most advanced version: **Introduced in Senate (IS)**
- Source file: `failed-legislation/118th-congress-s2773-ban-congressional-stock-trading-act-is.xml`
- Legislative progress:
  - Introduced in Senate
  - Referred to Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee
  - No committee report and no floor action
- How far it got: **Referral stage only.**
- Why it failed:
  - It did not advance out of committee (no hearing/markup/report that produced floor consideration).
  - It expired at the end of the 118th Congress without further action.
