# The Stock Amendment: Constitutional Convention Plan

A concrete step-by-step plan to add a constitutional amendment banning
congressional stock trading via an Article V constitutional convention.

---

## Premise

Congress has a structural conflict of interest: it would be regulating its own
members' personal financial behavior. The STOCK Act (2012) requires only
disclosure — it does not ban trading. Multiple stronger reform bills have
stalled. The Article V convention mechanism exists precisely for situations
where the national legislature will not act against its own interests.

**Goal:** Ratify a constitutional amendment prohibiting members of Congress from
directly or indirectly owning, purchasing, or selling individual securities
while in office.

**Path:** Article V state-driven constitutional convention, bypassing Congress
as the proposing body.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundational Work

### Step 1: Draft the Amendment Text

Produce precise, legally reviewed constitutional language before any public
campaign launches. The text must be:

- **Narrow** — limited to congressional securities trading
- **Unambiguous** — no room for Congress to redefine the issue when it feels
  pressure
- **Self-enforcing by reference** — includes a clause authorizing Congress to
  pass implementing legislation (enforcement mechanism)
- **Clear on scope** — must address members' spouses and dependent households
  to close obvious loopholes

**Draft concept:**

> *Members of Congress shall not directly or indirectly own, purchase, or sell
> individual securities while serving in office. All such assets must be
> divested or placed in a qualified diversified fund or blind trust within 180
> days of taking office. Congress shall enforce this article by appropriate
> legislation.*

This text must be finalized by constitutional law experts before any state
resolution is introduced. Identical language across all 34 state applications
is essential.

---

### Step 2: Draft the Convention Call Resolution

Simultaneously with the amendment text, draft the model state legislative
resolution that:

1. Invokes Article V of the United States Constitution
2. Requests a convention **strictly limited** to proposing the pre-drafted
   amendment text or its functional equivalent
3. Includes limiting language that the state's delegates are bound only to that
   topic
4. Specifies recall procedures if delegates exceed their authority

**Purpose:** Neutralize the "runaway convention" objection — the single largest
source of "no" votes from otherwise sympathetic legislators.

---

### Step 3: Draft Companion Delegate-Binding Legislation

Each state passing a convention resolution should also pass companion
legislation specifying:

- How the state selects its delegates
- That delegates are legally bound to the convention's single topic
- Recall mechanisms for delegates who violate their instructions
- Criminal or civil penalties for acting outside the mandate

This further reduces the risk profile of the convention call and gives
risk-averse legislators political cover to vote yes.

---

## Phase 2 — Organizational Build

### Step 4: Form the Coalition

Establish a named, single-issue coalition organization (or backbone entity)
with the following characteristics:

- Explicitly nonpartisan board and public leadership
- Mission statement focused **only** on this amendment — no scope expansion
- Includes representation from:
  - Anti-corruption and government ethics organizations
  - Article V convention procedural experts
  - Constitutional law scholars
  - Former members of Congress from both parties

See [OPERATIONS.md](OPERATIONS.md) for candidate organizations and coalition
structure.

---

### Step 5: Recruit Congressional Allies as Spokespersons

Identify and recruit sitting or former members of Congress from both parties
who publicly support a stock-trading ban and are willing to:

- State explicitly that **Congress cannot credibly regulate itself**
- Endorse the Article V convention path as the constitutionally correct remedy
- Provide insider testimony on how reform attempts are diluted internally
- Appear in media and at state legislative hearings

**Why this matters:** If sitting members of Congress say "Congress won't fix
this — the states must," it removes the narrative that the campaign is
anti-government or partisan. It becomes bipartisan institutional acknowledgment
of a constitutional problem.

**Discipline requirement:** These allies must publicly commit to supporting only
the exact pre-drafted amendment text and opposing congressional substitutes that
water down the language.

---

## Phase 3 — State Legislative Campaign

### Step 6: Identify and Tier Target States

**Threshold needed:** 34 states to call the convention; 38 to ratify.

Categorize all 50 states into tiers:

| Tier | Criteria | Target Count |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Tier 1 — Early adopters** | Strong anti-Washington sentiment, accessible legislatures, bipartisan reform track record | ~12–15 states |
| **Tier 2 — Persuadable** | Competitive legislatures, strong public opinion, some political risk | ~12–15 states |
| **Tier 3 — Hard targets** | Deeply partisan, institutionally resistant, or with strong congressional delegation opposition | Remaining |

Priority targeting: smaller, more accessible state legislatures first — they
are easier to organize politically and early wins create momentum.

---

### Step 7: Build State-Level Operations

Do not rely solely on national coordination. Each target state requires:

- A state-based point organization or partner
- Identification of at least one legislative champion per chamber
- A state-specific lobbying and constituent-pressure operation
- Relationships with local media

Run state campaigns **simultaneously** rather than sequentially. Early-mover
states encourage fence-sitters.

---

### Step 8: Launch the Public Campaign

Simultaneously with state legislative outreach:

- Launch national media campaign focused on the "Congress cannot regulate
  itself" narrative
- Publicize congressional allies' statements
- Run voter contact programs in Tier 1 and Tier 2 state legislative districts
- Maintain public tracking of state application progress ("X of 34 states")
- Activate coalition organizations' existing membership bases

**Central message discipline:** The issue must remain exactly one sentence —
*"Members of Congress should not trade individual stocks while in office."*
Nothing else.

---

### Step 9: Pass Applications in 34 States

Work state by state to secure passage of identical convention call resolutions.

Key milestones to amplify publicly:
- First 10 states (proof of concept)
- 17 states (one-third threshold — national media moment)
- 25 states (tipping point — Congress begins to feel genuine pressure)
- 30+ states (likely triggers preemptive congressional action)

**If Congress proposes the exact pre-drafted amendment text:** Evaluate whether
to pause state campaign and support ratification, or continue to ensure the
convention route as a backstop. The key test is whether congressional language
matches the pre-drafted text **exactly**. Any weakening or scope-limiting of
the amendment means the state campaign continues.

---

## Phase 4 — Convention

### Step 10: Congress Issues the Convention Call

Once 34 state applications are submitted, Congress is constitutionally required
to call the convention. The coalition must:

- Monitor the call for any procedural manipulation
- Insist on convention rules consistent with the limiting language in state
  resolutions
- Prepare legal challenges if Congress attempts to improperly restrict or
  reshape the convention

---

### Step 11: Convene and Propose the Amendment

The convention convenes with delegates bound by their state instructions.

The convention's sole action: vote to propose the pre-drafted amendment text
(or a version that does not weaken it).

The coalition maintains a public presence throughout to prevent scope creep.

---

## Phase 5 — Ratification

### Step 12: Ratification Campaign in 38 States

Once the convention proposes the amendment, the ratification campaign begins.

**Threshold:** 38 of 50 states (three-fourths).

Ratification politics are favorable because:

- State legislators are not restricting themselves — they are restricting federal
  officials
- "We fixed Washington" is a powerful political narrative in every state
- Public support for the underlying reform is 75–90% nationally

Congress determines the ratification method (state legislatures or state
ratifying conventions). The campaign should advocate for state legislatures,
where the existing infrastructure is already built.

---

### Step 13: The Amendment is Ratified

Upon ratification by 38 states, the amendment becomes part of the United States
Constitution.

Congress must pass implementing enforcement legislation within a set period.
The coalition monitors and advocates for strong implementing legislation that
does not undercut the amendment's intent.

---

## Summary Timeline (Estimated)

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Phase 1 — Foundational Work | 6–12 months |
| Phase 2 — Organizational Build | 6–12 months (overlaps Phase 1) |
| Phase 3 — State Campaign | 2–5 years |
| Phase 4 — Convention | 1–2 years |
| Phase 5 — Ratification | 1–3 years |

**Total elapsed time:** 4–10 years. Constitutional amendment movements operate
on long timelines. The 17th Amendment (direct election of senators) took
roughly a decade from organized state pressure to ratification.

---

## Key Dependencies

1. **Amendment text finalized first** — everything depends on identical,
   legally sound language
2. **Coalition discipline** — no scope expansion; one issue only
3. **Congressional allies** — essential for legitimacy and media access
4. **Funding** — multi-year, multi-state operations require significant
   sustained investment
5. **First-mover states** — early wins determine whether momentum forms
