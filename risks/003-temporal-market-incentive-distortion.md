# Risk 003: Temporal Market Incentive Distortion

**Priority:** High  
**Phase:** In-office governance and policy decision-making  
**Type:** Structural incentive / policy-quality risk

---

## Description

Even if members are fully restricted to broad domestic market exposure (no
individual stocks, no sector bets), a conflict can remain: elected officials
may still have personal incentives to prefer policies that lift near-term broad
market returns while imposing long-term economic drag.

This is a time-horizon conflict, not a single-security conflict. The risk is
that portfolio-relevant outcomes are realized quickly, while national costs are
deferred and distributed over time.

Potential manifestations include:
- Preference for policy designs that support short-term valuations over
  long-term productivity
- Underweighting of long-lag investments whose benefits arrive after current
  office terms
- Greater tolerance for hidden tail risk when immediate market response is
  positive

The core danger is not necessarily corruption in the criminal sense. It is
systematic bias in policy choice caused by misaligned incentive horizons.

---

## Why This Is a Legitimate Risk

Conflict-of-interest risk does not require proof that any specific vote was
caused by any specific investment gain. Institutional ethics design is based on
incentive structure and foreseeable bias channels.

A broad-fund-only regime removes company- and sector-specific conflicts, but it
can still leave market-level timing incentives intact. If governance design
ignores this channel, the reform may reduce scandals while preserving a weaker
form of self-interest distortion.

---

## Mitigation

### 1. Reduce Short-Term Sensitivity During Service

Use an in-office compliant vehicle whose crediting rule is not directly tied to
short-interval equity marks. Options include fixed annual crediting or
benchmark-linked crediting with smoothing and caps.

### 2. Delay Realization

Restrict participant access to in-office performance changes until after service
plus a cooling-off period. This lowers immediate personal payoff from short-run
policy-market interactions.

### 3. Smooth Returns Over Multi-Year Windows

Calculate crediting using multi-year averaging rather than annual or quarterly
marks. Smoothing weakens incentives to optimize for near-term market moves.

### 4. Eliminate In-Service Timing Discretion

No participant-directed reallocations, tactical shifts, or partial transfer
windows while serving. Mechanical, rules-based operation only.

### 5. Add Post-Service Accountability Tools

Allow statutory clawback or forfeiture mechanisms for gains tied to proven
ethics violations or concealment, with due process protections.

---

## Warning Signs

- Public messaging declares the conflict solved solely because individual stocks
  are banned, with no mention of time-horizon incentives
- Implementing legislation allows frequent in-service reallocations or timing
  choices by participants
- Crediting methodology is highly sensitive to short-term market moves
- No cooling-off period exists between leaving office and access to in-office
  performance accruals
- Debate framing collapses into scandal prevention only, with no policy-quality
  lens
